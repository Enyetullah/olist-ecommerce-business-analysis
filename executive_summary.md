# Executive Summary

This project analyzes Olist e-commerce marketplace data to understand revenue performance, customer behavior, seller performance, product category trends, delivery efficiency, payment behavior, and customer satisfaction.

The analysis found that the marketplace generated approximately $15.42M in delivered-order revenue from about 96K delivered orders. Revenue grew strongly through 2017, peaked in November 2017, and remained strong through much of 2018. Revenue was concentrated in a few major product categories, especially health_beauty, watches_gifts, and bed_bath_table. São Paulo was the strongest customer market and also the dominant seller state.

Customer satisfaction was generally positive, with an average review score of 4.16. However, delivery performance had a strong relationship with customer satisfaction. On-time orders had an average review score of 4.29, while late orders averaged only 2.57. This suggests that late delivery is one of the most important operational issues affecting customer experience.

The analysis also found that most customers were one-time buyers, with a repeat customer rate of only 3.12%. This shows a major opportunity to improve retention through loyalty programs, follow-up offers, and better post-purchase engagement.

Recommended business actions include improving delivery performance in high-risk states and slower categories, monitoring top sellers and high-revenue categories, and developing strategies to convert high-value one-time buyers into repeat customers.
