# Business Insights from Core SQL Analysis

## 1. Executive KPI Summary

The business had 96,478 delivered orders from 93,358 unique customers, generating $15,419,773.75 in total revenue. The average order value was $159.83, which gives a strong baseline for understanding customer spending behavior.

The average review score was 4.16 out of 5, showing generally positive customer satisfaction. However, the late delivery rate was 8.11%, so delivery performance is still an important area to monitor.

## 2. Order Status Distribution

Most orders were successfully delivered, with delivered orders making up 97.02% of all orders. This shows that the marketplace had a high order completion rate.

Canceled, unavailable, processing, and other unfinished statuses made up a small percentage of total orders. Even though these categories are small, they should still be monitored because they can affect customer experience and operational reliability.

## 3. Monthly Revenue Trend

Revenue grew strongly from early 2017 and reached its highest point in November 2017, with $1,153,364.20 in monthly revenue. This suggests that the platform experienced a major sales peak during that month.

Revenue remained relatively strong through much of 2018, especially from March to July, where monthly revenue stayed around or above $1 million. This shows more stable sales activity after the platform grew.

## 4. Monthly Order Volume Trend

Order volume followed a similar pattern to revenue, with the highest number of orders in November 2017 at 7,544 orders. This suggests the revenue peak was mainly driven by a higher number of orders rather than only higher order value.

The dataset shows very low order counts in September and October 2018, which may indicate incomplete data for those months. Because of that, those months should not be treated as normal full-month performance.

## 5. Revenue by Customer State

São Paulo generated the most revenue, with $5,769,703.15 from 40,501 orders. This means SP is the strongest customer market in the dataset and should be a key focus for business and logistics planning.

The top three customer states, SP, RJ, and MG, together generated a large share of total revenue. This shows that customer demand is heavily concentrated in a few major regions.

## 6. Top Customer Cities by Revenue

São Paulo city generated the highest revenue, with $2,107,960.17 from 15,045 orders. Rio de Janeiro was second with $1,111,732.21, showing that large urban areas drive much of the marketplace revenue.

The top cities are mostly major population and business centers. This suggests that marketing, seller support, and delivery optimization should focus strongly on these high-volume urban markets.

## 7. Revenue by Product Category

The highest revenue-generating category was health_beauty, with $1,441,248.07 in revenue. Watches_gifts and bed_bath_table were also major revenue drivers, showing strong demand in lifestyle and household-related categories.

Bed_bath_table had the highest order count among the top categories, but its average review score was lower than health_beauty and watches_gifts. This suggests that high sales volume does not always mean stronger customer satisfaction.

## 8. Product Category Performance with Delivery and Reviews

Health_beauty performed strongly across revenue, order volume, and customer satisfaction, with $1,412,089.53 in revenue and an average review score of 4.19. This makes it one of the strongest overall categories.

Office_furniture stood out as a weaker category because it had the lowest average review score at 3.51 and the longest average delivery time at 20.84 days. This suggests delivery or fulfillment issues may be hurting customer satisfaction in that category.

## 9. Top Sellers by Revenue

The top seller generated $247,007.06 in revenue from 1,124 orders, showing that individual sellers can contribute significantly to marketplace performance.

Some high-revenue sellers had noticeably lower review scores. For example, one top seller generated $237,806.69 but had an average review score of 3.35, suggesting that revenue performance should be evaluated alongside customer satisfaction.

## 10. Seller State Performance

São Paulo was the dominant seller state, with 1,769 sellers, 68,641 orders, and $9,957,056.91 in revenue. This shows that seller activity is highly concentrated in SP.

SP generated about 64.6% of seller-side revenue, making it the main supply hub in the dataset. This concentration is useful for efficiency, but it also means business performance may depend heavily on sellers from one region.

## 11. Payment Type Breakdown

Credit card was the dominant payment method, generating $12,542,084.19 in payment value. This shows that most customer spending on the platform was handled through credit card payments.

Boleto was the second-largest payment method, generating $2,869,361.27. Even though credit card is the main payment option, boleto still represents an important customer payment preference.

## 12. Payment Installment Behavior

One-time payments had the highest total payment value at $5,907,233.36, showing that many customers preferred paying in full. However, installment payments also made up a large share of payment value.

Orders with 7–12 installments had a much higher average payment value of $333.29. This suggests customers may use longer installment plans for more expensive purchases.

## 13. Review Score Distribution

Most reviews were positive, with 57.78% of reviews receiving a score of 5. This supports the overall average review score of 4.16 and shows generally strong customer satisfaction.

However, 1-star and 2-star reviews together made up 14.69% of all reviews. These negative reviews are important because they may point to delivery problems, product issues, or seller performance concerns.

## 14. Average Review Score by Month

Average review scores were generally around 4.0 or higher for most full months, showing stable customer satisfaction over time. June 2018 had one of the strongest monthly scores at 4.28.

There were noticeable drops in February and March 2018, with March 2018 reaching 3.75. These lower-scoring months should be compared with delivery performance, order volume, and seller activity to understand what caused the decline.

## 15. Late Delivery Impact on Review Score

On-time orders had an average review score of 4.29, while late orders had an average review score of only 2.57. This shows a strong negative relationship between late delivery and customer satisfaction.

Late orders also took much longer to arrive, averaging 31.38 days compared to 10.88 days for on-time orders. This suggests delivery delays are one of the most important operational issues affecting customer experience.

## 16. Delivery Performance by Customer State

Alagoas had the highest late delivery rate at 23.93%, followed by Maranhão at 19.67%. These states may need closer investigation because customers there experienced more delivery problems.

São Paulo had a much lower late delivery rate of 5.89% and an average delivery time of 8.76 days. This suggests delivery performance was much stronger in the platform’s largest customer market.

## 17. Average Delivery Time by Product Category

Office_furniture had the longest average delivery time at 20.84 days and also had the lowest average review score at 3.51. This makes it a clear category for operational improvement.

Books_imported had the fastest average delivery time at 8.13 days and a strong review score of 4.51. This suggests that faster delivery is generally connected with better customer experience.

## 18. Repeat Customer Rate

Only 2,997 out of 96,096 customers placed more than one order, giving a repeat customer rate of 3.12%. This shows that most customers in the dataset were one-time buyers.

The average number of orders per customer was only 1.03. This suggests customer retention is a major opportunity for improvement through loyalty programs, follow-up promotions, or better post-purchase engagement.

## 19. Top Customers by Spending

The highest-spending customer spent $13,664.08 in a single order. Several of the top customers also made only one purchase, showing that high revenue can come from large one-time purchases.

Because many top spenders are not repeat customers, the business may be missing opportunities to retain high-value buyers. These customers could be targeted with follow-up offers or loyalty campaigns.

## 20. High Revenue but Low Satisfaction Categories

Watches_gifts, bed_bath_table, computers_accessories, and furniture_decor generated high revenue but had lower average review scores compared with stronger-performing categories. These categories are important because they bring in revenue but may also create customer satisfaction risks.

Office_furniture is especially concerning because it had both low satisfaction and long delivery time, with an average review score of 3.51 and average delivery time of 20.82 days. This category should be prioritized for delivery and seller-performance improvement.
