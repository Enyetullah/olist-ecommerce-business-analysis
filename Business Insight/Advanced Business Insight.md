# Advanced Business Insights

## 1. Month-over-Month Revenue Growth

Revenue showed strong growth during 2017, especially in February 2017, when revenue increased by 112.77% compared with the previous month. November 2017 was also a major growth month, with revenue increasing by 53.55% and order volume increasing by 62.77%.

The largest meaningful decline happened in December 2017, when revenue dropped by 26.90% after the November 2017 peak. This suggests that November may have been a seasonal or promotional sales spike, and the business should compare this period with campaign activity or holiday shopping behavior.

## 2. Three-Month Moving Average Revenue

The three-month moving average shows that revenue became much more stable in 2018. The highest three-month moving average occurred in May 2018 at $1,127,417.23, showing that the business maintained strong revenue performance over several months.

This smoother trend is useful because it reduces the effect of one-month spikes. Even though November 2017 had the highest single-month revenue, the moving average shows that early-to-mid 2018 was the strongest sustained revenue period.

## 3. Top Product Categories by Customer State

Health_beauty was the most common top-performing category across customer states. It ranked as the number one revenue category in 15 states and appeared in the top three categories in 24 states.

The strongest category varied by major state. In São Paulo, bed_bath_table ranked first; in Rio de Janeiro, watches_gifts ranked first; and in Minas Gerais, health_beauty ranked first. This shows that product demand is not the same in every region, so regional category trends should be considered when planning marketing or inventory decisions.

## 4. Seller Revenue Ranking by State

The highest-ranked seller overall came from São Paulo and generated $247,007.06 in revenue from 1,124 orders. Other high-performing top sellers came from Bahia, Rio de Janeiro, and Minas Gerais, showing that strong sellers exist across multiple states.

However, seller revenue is still heavily concentrated in a few states, especially São Paulo. This suggests that marketplace supply performance depends strongly on sellers from specific regions, and the business should monitor whether seller concentration creates operational risk.

## 5. Delivery Speed and Review Scores

Faster deliveries were linked with better review scores. Orders delivered within 0–3 days had an average review score of 4.48, while orders in slower delivery groups had lower average review scores.

This supports the earlier finding that delivery performance affects customer satisfaction. The business should prioritize reducing long delivery times because slower delivery is associated with weaker customer reviews and a higher late delivery rate.

## 6. Customer Segmentation by Spending and Frequency

Most customers were one-time or low-value customers. This group included 86,661 customers, or about 92.83% of all customers, and generated $10,939,089.54 in revenue.

High-value one-time customers were a smaller group, with 3,896 customers, but they generated $3,616,496.75 in revenue. This suggests that many valuable customers only purchased once, which creates a major opportunity for retention campaigns, follow-up offers, and loyalty programs.

## 7. Category Revenue Contribution

Revenue was spread across multiple product categories, but the top categories still contributed a large share. The top five categories generated 39.26% of total revenue, while the top ten categories generated 62.40%.

Health_beauty was the largest category, contributing 9.16% of total revenue. Watches_gifts, bed_bath_table, sports_leisure, and computers_accessories were also major revenue contributors, so these categories should be closely monitored for sales trends, delivery performance, and customer satisfaction.

## 8. Seller Concentration Analysis

The top seller generated 1.60% of total revenue, and the top 10 sellers together generated 12.93% of total revenue. The top 20 sellers generated 21.05% of total revenue.

This shows that revenue is somewhat concentrated among top sellers, but not completely dependent on one seller. The business should still monitor top sellers closely because their performance has a noticeable impact on total marketplace revenue.

## 9. Monthly Review Score and Late Delivery Trend

March 2018 had the highest meaningful late delivery rate at 21.06% and one of the lowest average review scores at 3.81. February 2018 also had a high late delivery rate of 15.89% and a low average review score of 3.88.

This strengthens the finding that delivery issues are connected to customer satisfaction. Months with higher late delivery rates generally had weaker review scores, so delivery performance should be tracked as a key customer experience metric.

## 10. Categories With Highest Late Delivery Rate

Audio had the highest late delivery rate at 12.71%, followed by fashion_underwear_beach at 12.60% and christmas_supplies at 12.00%. These categories may need closer review because customers experienced more frequent delivery delays.

Office_furniture is also an important concern. Even though its late delivery rate was not the highest, it had the lowest average review score at 3.51 and the longest average delivery time at 20.84 days. This suggests that office_furniture may have broader fulfillment or customer satisfaction problems beyond only late deliveries.

## Advanced Analysis Summary

The advanced analysis shows that the business had strong revenue growth and stable performance during 2018, but customer retention and delivery performance are major improvement areas. Most customers purchased only once, while late deliveries and slower delivery times were linked with lower review scores.

The strongest business opportunities are improving customer retention, monitoring high-revenue categories, supporting top sellers, and reducing delivery delays in weaker categories and regions.
